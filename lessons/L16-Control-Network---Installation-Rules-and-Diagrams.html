L16 – Control Network: Installation Rules and Diagrams

Source: DeltaV M-series Hardware Installation and Reference Manual, Emerson D800001X312, April 2021 — Appendix G, Sections G.5.9–G.6.5

1. Overview

This lesson provides a reference of supported network installation diagrams for the DeltaV Control Network, covering Cisco 3750, 2960, and DeltaV Smart Switch topologies. Each diagram includes numbered callouts that define cabling, grounding, and configuration requirements.

Key Rule — Duplex Auto-Negotiation: All twisted pair ports are configured to auto-sense speed and auto-negotiate duplex. Never hard-configure speed or duplex on twisted pair switch ports — duplex mismatches cause communications failures.

2. Universal Cabling Rules

Every network diagram references these core cabling callout descriptions. Memorize them — they apply across all topologies.

CalloutApplies ToDescription
1 Switch-to-switch (twisted pair) 100 m (max) straight-through or crossover cable. Cat. 5(e) Screened Twisted Pair cable is required between switches.
2 Switch-to-switch (ground loop prevention) Build with a shielded, metal-enclosed RJ45 connector on one end and an isolated, plastic-enclosed RJ45 connector on the other. The metal connector end can be placed on either switch.
3 Controller-to-switch 100 m (max) straight-through cable. The controller's RJ45 shield connects only to a Faraday cage (not DC ground) — connectors are floating. Build with shielded, metal-enclosed RJ45 connectors on both ends.
4 Workstation-to-switch 100 m (max) straight-through cable. To prevent ground loops, build with shielded metal on switch end, plastic on PC end. Metal connector end must be on the switch, not the PC.
5 / 7 Switch-to-switch (fiber, 100Mbit) 2 km (max) full duplex Multimode Fiber with MTRJ connectors.
6 Switch management A dedicated non-DeltaV PC is required for switch management via telnet or web browser.
8 / 10 Cisco stacking A Cisco stacking cable connects two 3750-series switches into a single managed unit with one IP address.
Critical Warning — Ground Loops: The controller's RJ45 shield is floating (Faraday cage only, not DC ground). The single point of ground is at the hub/switch. Mixing shield types on the wrong ends creates ground loops that cause intermittent communications failures.

Design Principles Recap:
  • Always use screened twisted pair between switches
  • Metal/plastic RJ45 combinations prevent ground loops
  • Controller shields are floating — ground at the switch
  • Never hard-configure speed/duplex on TP ports
  • Use gigabit links for switch-to-switch, 100Mbit for edge devices
  • Lock down unused ports after installation
  • Maximum 6 switches in series in Smart Switch TP networks

7. Quiz

Knowledge Check

Q1: When building a cable assembly to connect a DeltaV controller to a switch, how should the RJ45 connectors be configured, and why?

A: Shielded metal-enclosed RJ45 on both ends. The controller's shield connects only to a Faraday cage (floating), so the single point of ground is at the switch.

Q2: What happens if you hard-configure speed or duplex on a twisted pair switch port in a DeltaV system?

A: Duplex mismatches will occur, creating communications failures. All TP ports must auto-sense and auto-negotiate.

Q3: How many switches can be in series between any two devices in a DeltaV Smart Switch twisted pair network?

A: No more than six switches.
