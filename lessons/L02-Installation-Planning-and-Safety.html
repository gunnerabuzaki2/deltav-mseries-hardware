L02: Installation Planning and Safety

Source: DeltaV Scalable Process System — Hardware Installation and Reference Manual, D800001X312, April 2021 (Emerson). Chapter 2, "Installing Your DeltaV System," pp. 23–27.

You know what the system looks like from L01. Now it is time to talk about what happens before anything gets mounted on a rail. Installation planning and safety are not glamorous topics, but they are the topics that determine whether your system runs for twenty years or fails in its first year. This lesson covers site preparation, regulatory compliance, wiring and power guidelines, torque limits, and the eight-step installation sequence.

2.1 — Regulatory Compliance: CE, RoHS, and Hazardous Areas

Before a single DIN rail is bolted to a backplate, you need to understand the regulatory environment your installation lives in. Emerson's DeltaV hardware has been tested for compliance with several directives, and the manual is explicit: to maintain compliance, products must be installed and maintained according to the documented procedures.

Key Concept — CE Compliance Is Not Optional: The manual states that installation and maintenance must follow the procedures in this document. Failure to do so may compromise CE compliance. This is not just a paperwork issue — it affects whether your system is legally deployable in the European Economic Area.

China RoHS

DeltaV products manufactured on or after 1 July 2016 and shipped into China are China RoHS compliant. You can verify product certificates at the Emerson documentation website.

Tip — Marine Installations Have Extra Requirements: Marine-certified DeltaV installations require IP66/NEMA 4 enclosures with EMC-reduction shielding of at least 15 dB. The enclosure door must remain closed during normal operation — opening it defeats both the EMC shielding and electrostatic discharge protection. You also need an EMC power line filter (such as a Tyco S Series) on all DC input leads.
Pitfall — AC I/O Cards Have Hazardous Live Voltages: The AC input and output I/O cards and carriers may have hazardous live voltages on their terminals — 120 VAC or 250 VAC. Always de-energize field power before installation, maintenance, or wiring changes on these devices. The affected devices include specific carriers (KJ4001X1-BE1, KJ4002X1, KJ4003X1) and Series 2 AC cards (KJ3207X1, KJ3209X1, KJ3210X1).

2.3 — Tools, Site Preparation, and Wiring Guidelines

2.3.1 — Tools Required

You need three categories of tools:

  • Standard electrical tools: voltmeter, wire cutter, wire stripper, pliers, screwdriver
  • Standard installation tools: screwdrivers, drill with standard bits
  • Ethernet cable tools: crimper, cable tester

2.3.2 — Site Preparation

Key Concept — The Electrical Foundation Matters Most: The manual says it plainly: "Even the best control system can only perform as well as the electrical foundation on which it is built." Clean input power, single-point grounding, and adequate shielding from outside electrical interference are non-negotiable requirements.

Power distribution, ground networks, and signal wiring should be installed before the DeltaV system itself. Emerson recommends using the separate manual DeltaV Power and Grounding as your resource for proper site preparation.

2.3.3 — Wiring Guidelines

The power, grounding, and field termination points on the I/O subsystem and system power supplies are designed to accept 2.5 mm² (14 AWG) stranded or solid wire. Select your wire size by calculating the maximum current limit for each wire, then apply local electrical code requirements.

For low-level signal wiring, shielded twisted pair wiring is recommended to reduce noise susceptibility. I/O carriers are available with or without a shield bar — choose the version that matches your field wiring termination needs.

Pitfall — No Bullet-Style Wiring: Some images in the manual show bullet-style wiring connections. This is for reference only. Emerson never recommends splicing wires into wires to emulate bullet connections. Always use terminal-to-terminal wiring.

2.3.4 — Power Guidelines

The removable connectors on System Power Supplies and legacy Bulk Power Supplies are not designed to interrupt current flow in circuits under load. If you need to disconnect power, use a separate component — such as a knife blade fuse terminal block or breaker — that is designed for this purpose.

Pitfall — Never Unplug a Connector Under Load: Pulling a power connector while current is flowing can damage the connector and potentially the power supply. Always use a dedicated disconnect device (breaker, fuse block) in the supply circuit.

2.4 — Torque Limits

Over-tightening or under-torquing mounting screws and termination screws can cause mechanical damage, poor electrical contact, or both. The following table gives you the exact limits:

Tip — Buy a Torque Screwdriver: Most of the DeltaV components require very low torque (0.11 Nm = 1 in-lb). A calibrated torque screwdriver in the 0.1–3.0 Nm range is inexpensive insurance against stripped threads or cracked PCBs.

2.5 — The Eight-Step Installation Sequence

The manual lays out eight installation steps in the most common order. You may reorder them for your project, but understanding this sequence helps you plan the workflow:

  1. Install DIN rails and carriers — mount the rails, attach power/controller and I/O interface carriers.
  2. Install I/O interface — set key settings on terminal blocks, install them on the carrier, connect field wiring, then install I/O cards.
  3. Install the controller — mount it on the power/controller carrier.
  4. Install the system power supply — mount it on the power/controller carrier and connect supply inputs.
  5. Install workstations and servers — follow manufacturer instructions, install the system identifier (USB), set up printer and UPS.
  6. Set up the Control Network — install hubs/switches and run network cables between nodes.
  7. Connect power and power up — install the bulk power supply, connect supply inputs, and power on the system.
  8. Check out the installation — verify cable connections, check power supply voltages, inspect LED indicators, and test field wiring.
Key Concept — Cooling Requires Vertical Mounting: I/O cards and controllers must be mounted vertically on horizontal carriers for cooling purposes. This is a caution in the manual — not following it leads to thermal damage.

Summary — What to Remember

  • Regulatory compliance is the first step — CE, RoHS, and hazardous area requirements must be addressed before installation.
  • The electrical foundation (clean power, single-point ground, shielding) is the most critical factor in system reliability.
  • Use 2.5 mm² (14 AWG) wire for power and ground terminations; use shielded twisted pair for low-level signals.
  • Never disconnect a power connector under load — always use a dedicated breaker or fuse block.
  • Follow the torque limits table — most DeltaV components need only 0.11 Nm (1 in-lb).
  • The installation follows an eight-step sequence, with cooling requiring vertical card mounting on horizontal carriers.

Quiz — Test Your Understanding

Question 1

Why must I/O cards and controllers be mounted vertically on horizontal carriers?

Show Answer

For cooling purposes. Horizontal mounting prevents proper air convection and can lead to thermal damage of the components.

Question 2

What is the maximum torque you should apply to an I/O card mounting screw?

Show Answer

0.11 Nm (1 in-lb). This is the same limit for controllers, media converters, system power supplies, and terminal block protection covers.

Question 3

Why should you never disconnect a power connector while current is flowing?

Show Answer

The removable connectors on system power supplies and bulk power supplies are not designed to interrupt current flow under load. Disconnecting under load can damage the connector and potentially the power supply. Use a dedicated breaker or fuse block instead.

Question 4

What wire size is specified for power, grounding, and field termination points on the DeltaV I/O subsystem?

Show Answer

2.5 mm² (14 AWG) stranded or solid wire. Actual wire gauge should also meet local electrical code requirements for the expected current.

Question 5

What is the minimum EMC-reduction shielding required for marine DeltaV installations?

Show Answer

15 dB minimum, installed in an IP66/NEMA 4 enclosure (such as the Stahl Series 8125/8126). Marine certification is approved for 24 V powered systems only.
