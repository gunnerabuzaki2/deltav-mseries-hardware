8.1 Reading the LEDs: A Universal Approach

Every DeltaV hardware device — from the simplest I/O card to the most complex controller — communicates its health status through a small set of front-panel LEDs. Learning to read these LEDs is one of the most valuable skills in DeltaV maintenance. The good news is that almost every device follows a consistent pattern:

Key Concept — The Universal LED Pattern:
  • Green Power LED — Should be solid On when the device is powered. If it is Off, check power supply and connections.
  • Red Error LED — Should be Off during normal operation. If it is On (solid or flashing), the device has a fault that needs investigation.
  • Yellow/Amber LEDs — Indicate channel-level, port-level, or network-level status. Their meaning depends on the specific card type.
When diagnosing any DeltaV hardware issue, start by looking at the Green and Red LEDs. If Power is On and Error is Off, the device itself is likely healthy — the problem is probably upstream (controller not scanning) or downstream (field wiring).

The most common cause is an output overload — too many I/O cards drawing too much current. Before replacing the supply, verify that the total load on the unit is within its rated capacity. Key Concept — Active vs. Standby LEDs: In a redundant controller pair, one controller is Active (running the control strategy) and the other is Standby (ready to take over). The Active LED is lit on whichever controller is currently executing control. If both controllers show Standby lit but neither shows Active, neither is running the control strategy — this is a critical fault that requires immediate attention.
⚠️ Common Misdiagnosis: A flashing Red Error LED on a controller is sometimes mistaken for a hardware failure, but it typically means the controller is decommissioned. Before calling technical support, check whether the controller has been commissioned in DeltaV Explorer. A quick commission usually resolves this.

8.23 Quick Reference: Red Error LED Diagnosis Flowchart

When you see a Red Error LED on any DeltaV device, follow this decision tree:

Red Error LED StateMost Likely CauseFirst Action
On (continuous)Unit failed self-test or controller not scanning cardCheck controller operation first; if controller is healthy, contact technical support
FlashingAddress conflict or controller not scanning card (pre-Series 2); no bussed field power or hardware error (Series 2)For pre-Series 2: check for duplicate addresses
For Series 2: check bussed field power first
On for 1 sec, then all LEDs on for 5 secUnrecoverable software error (controllers and RIUs only)Contact customer support
Flashing (on controller/RIU only)Device is decommissionedCommission the device in DeltaV Explorer
Key Concept — Series 2 vs. pre-Series 2 Error Behavior: The most important difference between Series 2 and pre-Series 2 cards is how they indicate the Red Error LED on flashing. On pre-Series 2 cards, a flashing Red Error typically indicates an address conflict. On Series 2 cards (especially redundant ones), a flashing Red Error more commonly indicates no bussed field power or a hardware error. Always know which card generation you are working with before diagnosing.

In this lesson, you learned the complete LED indicator reference for every major DeltaV M-series device: power supplies, controllers (MD Plus/MX/MQ), remote interface units, media converters, fiber switches, controller firewalls, and all I/O card types including AI (8-channel and 16-channel), AO, DI, DO (8-channel and 32-channel), Fieldbus H1, AS-Interface, DeviceNet, Profibus DP, Isolated Input, Multifunction, RTD/Thermocouple, Sequence of Events, and Serial cards. You also learned the universal LED pattern (Green Power, Red Error, Yellow channel indicators) and how to distinguish between Series 2 and pre-Series 2 error behavior. Quiz — Lesson 8

1. What are the three types of LEDs that appear on virtually every DeltaV I/O card, and what does each indicate?

Click to reveal answer

(1) Green Power LED — indicates the card has power. (2) Red Error LED — indicates a fault condition. (3) Yellow channel LEDs — indicate channel-level status (input state, output state, communication status, or fault conditions depending on card type).

2. A Red Error LED is flashing on a pre-Series 2 AI 8-Channel card. What is the most likely cause?

Click to reveal answer

An address conflict. On pre-Series 2 cards, a flashing Red Error typically indicates that two cards share the same address. Replace the card with a known good card of the same type and contact technical support.

3. A Series 2 redundant AI card has its Red Error LED flashing. What should you check first?

Click to reveal answer

Check bussed field power first. On Series 2 redundant cards, a flashing Red Error most commonly indicates no bussed field power. After fixing the problem, use the DeltaV Diagnostics "Clear Saved Fault Information" command.

4. What does it mean when the Power/Active LED on a Fieldbus H1 card is flashing (rather than solid On)?

Click to reveal answer

A flashing Power/Active LED on a Fieldbus H1 card indicates an incorrect terminal block installation. For example, a simplex terminal block may have been installed on a card configured for redundancy. Install the correct terminal block type for the card configuration.

5. On a controller, what is the difference between the Active and Standby LEDs being On vs. both being Off?

Click to reveal answer

In a redundant pair, the Active LED is lit on whichever controller is currently executing the control strategy. The Standby LED is lit on the backup controller. If both Active LEDs are off (meaning neither controller shows Active), neither is running the control strategy — this is a critical fault.

6. What should you do before replacing a power supply that shows a Red Error LED?

Click to reveal answer

Verify the output loading. A Red Error LED on a power supply often indicates outputs outside tolerance — meaning too much current is being drawn. Check your loading calculations to ensure the total load is within the unit's rated capacity before replacing the supply.

7. What does a controller's Red Error LED showing "On for one second, then all LEDs on for five seconds" indicate?

Click to reveal answer

The controller went through a RESET due to an unrecoverable software error. Contact customer support.

8. What does a flashing Red Error LED on a controller (as opposed to an I/O card) typically indicate?

Click to reveal answer

The controller is decommissioned. Commission the controller in DeltaV Explorer before investigating other causes.

9. On a Media Converter, what do the "F Lnk" and "C Lnk" LED suffixes stand for?

Click to reveal answer

"F Lnk" stands for Fiber Link (fiber-optic connection) and "C Lnk" stands for Copper Link (twisted-pair Ethernet connection). Both appear on the Primary and Secondary sides of the Media Converter.

10. On a Serial Card configured as Slave, what does a flashing Yellow port LED indicate?

Click to reveal answer

A flashing Yellow port LED on a Serial Card configured as Slave indicates intermittent communication. Check the connections, cable, and external device.
