1. Vertical Carrier Types Overview

Two Carrier Families: DeltaV vertical carriers come in two types — VerticalPLUS (for both basic process control and Safety Instrumented Systems) and Legacy Vertical (for basic process control only, not for SIS).

3. Installing VerticalPLUS Carriers

3.1 Installing the DIN Rail Stop

StepActionNotes
1Verify the arrow on the stop front points upward toward the deviceDownward arrows risk screw damage to device pins
2Hook the deeper-notched end over one side of the DIN rail, then hook the shallower notch over the other side—
3Screw the stop onto the DIN rail — do not overtightenBoth sides must be properly secured; observe torque limits
DIN Rail Stop Location: Always install at the lowest position on the DIN rail, before installing any carriers.

3.2 Installing and Removing Carriers

Installation Procedure:

StepAction
1Position carrier on DIN rail and push to snap into place
2Slide carrier down until it meets the DIN rail stop
3Snap next carrier onto the DIN rail
4Connect adjacent carriers by sliding pins together
5Connect ground wiring (one ground wire per group of carriers on a DIN rail)

Removal Procedure:

  1. Use the largest flat-blade screwdriver that fits through the latch access holes
  2. Turn the latch cam approximately one-quarter turn to the right (as indicated on the carrier top graphic)

4. Extending LocalBus Power

When VerticalPLUS carriers are on separate DIN rails, carrier extender cables extend LocalBus power through 1-wide VerticalPLUS carrier extenders.

4.1 Power Extended from Left to Right Carriers (6-Carrier System)

StepAction
1Connect left 1-wide extender to last 8-wide carrier on left side (carrier 3) via 96-pin connectors
2Connect right 1-wide extender to first 8-wide carrier on right side (carrier 4) via 96-pin connectors
3Connect 25-pin D-shell cable to D-shell connector labeled A on left extender; fasten retainer screws
4Connect other end of D-shell cable to D-shell connector labeled A on right extender; fasten retainer screws

4.2 Power Extended from Right to Left Carriers (Up to 8 Carriers)

StepAction
1Connect left 1-wide extender (or SISNet Repeater carrier) to last carrier on right side (carrier 6)
2Connect right 1-wide extender to first carrier on left side (carrier 7)
3Connect 25-pin D-shell cable to D-shell labeled A on right extender/SISNet Repeater
4Connect other end to D-shell labeled A on left extender; fasten screws

5. Inserting System Power

When the total power draw exceeds the power supply's capability at a given position:

  1. Calculate power per position on the carrier
  2. Identify the position where the limit is exceeded (count from the power supply end)
  3. Locate the power jumper just before that position
  4. Remove the jumper and insert additional 12 VDC power at that node
⚠ Warning: Removing the jumper removes all 12 VDC power to all cards on all downstream carriers. Cards will be unable to hold the last value during the power transition.
Grounding Rule: When multiple power supplies are used, ensure they are grounded at the same point.

7.3 Legacy Vertical Power Splitting

The 8-wide Legacy Vertical I/O carriers have a split high-side power plane with a shared common return plane. A jumper bridges both high-side planes. To add power:

  1. Calculate power per position on the carrier
  2. Find the position where the limit is exceeded (counting from the power supply end)
  3. Remove the jumper just before that position
  4. Insert additional 12 VDC power at the node
⚠ Right Carrier Caution: On right 8-wide Legacy Vertical carriers, removing the jumper only disconnects the 96-pin carrier connector — it does not disconnect the 48-pin cable connector. Be careful when power is extended to a right carrier with a connected cable.

8. Legacy Vertical System Length Calculation

Maximum LocalBus Length: 6.5 m (21.3 ft) — same for vertical and horizontal systems. This supports up to eight 8-wide carriers, one 4-wide carrier, one bottom cable extender, and one top cable extender.

Each carrier (C1–C8) = 22 inches. Carriers C6 and C7 are spurs (not counted in series length).

Review Questions

1. What is the maximum LocalBus length for both vertical and horizontal DeltaV systems?

6.5 m (21.3 ft), including all cabling.

2. Why must a DIN rail stop be installed before mounting vertical carriers?

Vibration causes vertical carriers to slip down the DIN rail, disconnecting from the carrier above. The stop prevents this slippage.

3. What happens when you remove a power jumper on a VerticalPLUS carrier?

All 12 VDC power is removed from all cards on all downstream carriers. Cards are unable to hold their last value.

4. In a Legacy Vertical system, why must you use the correct cable type (1m vs 2m)?

Using the wrong cable results in a corrupt addressing scheme. The 1m bottom cable connects left-to-right carriers at the bottom; the 2m top cable bridges right-to-left carriers at the top.

5. What is the significance of carriers C6 and C7 in an 8-carrier Legacy Vertical system length calculation?

They are spurs, not in series with the rest of the system. Their lengths are not counted in the overall LocalBus length calculation, but spur lengths must be ≤ total system length. No additional carriers may be connected to spurs.
